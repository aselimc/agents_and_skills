# Proposal Self-Review Checklist

Run through this checklist before delivering the final output. For a deeper review, use the `proposal-reviewer` skill.

## Consistency Checks

- [ ] Every objective maps to at least one work package
- [ ] Every work package maps to at least one objective
- [ ] Deliverable due dates fall within their WP's timeline
- [ ] Milestone dates are consistent with the Gantt chart
- [ ] Personnel effort totals match across WP descriptions and budget
- [ ] Partner names/acronyms are consistent throughout
- [ ] Project duration is consistent across all sections

## Content Quality

- [ ] Abstract is self-contained and under 300 words
- [ ] State of the art cites recent publications from reputable venues
- [ ] Objectives are specific and measurable (SMART)
- [ ] Methodology explains HOW, not just WHAT
- [ ] Risks include at least one high-impact item with mitigation
- [ ] Impact section has concrete exploitation plans, not just aspirations

## Overpromise & Legal Risk Flags

Review for these red flags and soften or remove:

- **Guarantee language**: "will certainly", "guaranteed to", "will definitely" → replace with "aims to", "is expected to", "targets"
- **Unrealistic quantitative claims**: Benchmark numbers without evidence → add "preliminary results suggest" or cite feasibility study
- **Binding commitments**: "we commit to delivering", "we will provide" without qualification → add "subject to", "contingent on"
- **Scope creep signals**: Objectives that exceed what the budget/timeline can realistically support
- **Missing caveats**: Performance claims without stating assumptions or test conditions
- **Overly broad impact claims**: "will revolutionize", "will transform the industry" → use measured language

## Formatting

- [ ] Section numbering is consistent
- [ ] Figures and tables are referenced in text
- [ ] References are complete (no "[?]" or missing entries)
- [ ] Page/word count meets the call's requirements (if specified)
- [ ] Font, margins, and spacing match call guidelines (if specified)
